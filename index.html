Frequency of Calls by Date

Each day was separated and calculated to be a "time of day". Many expect emergency calls to be at night, but in actuality, most calls were made in the afternoon. The significance of this is that emergency centers can have more people on staff during the afternoon period since more calls are at this time on any day of the week. Lunch breaks no longer, maybe have meal breaks after 3PM instead.

Types of Calls

Emergency calls for medical care hold a large margin, which indicates that emergency services should hire more medical practicioners. On the flip side, water rescue is of less importance, so budget for boats and nets should be cut down. This may be surprising to some, as the Bay Area is surrounded by water, hence the bay area name

Units and Response Times

Each unit was grouped and the average response time was calculated. It seems that Investigation takes a significantly longer time than the other units, most likely because it is not an emergency service. However, this is not an excuse for investigators to arrive to a scene 25 minutes later. This could mean starting training programs for support and investigators to see how to improve their response time.

How to shorten dispatch time

Here's what our analysis found

These are the locations that take the longest to dispatch to on average. It is unacceptable to take almost an hour to respond to a call. There is a call to improve the technology used for emergency reponse systems.

One technology that will be useful in the comings years is self driving technology. If emergency vehicles were self-driven, there would be a decrease in time for waiting for personnel to enter the vehicle. Once there is a mass use of autonomous vehicles, this will reduce traffic and accidents, saving precious minutes needed to potentially save a life

For the short term, something that can be done to optimize dispatch times is to automate what can be automated, including dispatch assignments. When 911 is called, use voice detection technology to detect the type of emergency. This way, there is no human error with entering data, and teams can be dispatched faster without the intermediary human.
